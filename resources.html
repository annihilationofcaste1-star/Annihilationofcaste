Books to read next

Curated for readers who want to understand caste beyond Ambedkar — through sociology, memoir, fiction, and contemporary scholarship.

Find the book ↗
Ants Among Elephants
Sujatha Gidla, 2017 — A Dalit family memoir from Andhra Pradesh. Rare and essential.

Becoming Babasaheb
Dhananjay Keer, 1954 — The definitive biography of Ambedkar.
Find the book ↗
The Prisons We Broke
Baby Kamble, 2008 — Memoir of Dalit life in Maharashtra. First-person witness to untouchability.
Find the book ↗
Joothan
Om Prakash Valmiki, 1997 — A Dalit autobiography from UP. The word means "leftover food scraps fed to lower castes." Raw, essential.
Find the book ↗
Ants Among Elephants
Sujatha Gidla, 2017 — A Dalit family memoir from Andhra Pradesh. The only English-language Dalit memoir from Telugu country.
Find the book ↗
A Part Apart
Ajay Navaria, 2015 — Hindi Dalit short fiction in translation. Urban, contemporary, unsparing.
Find the book ↗
Defying the Odds
Milind Awad, 2020 — How Dalit entrepreneurs navigate caste in Indian business. Rarely discussed economic dimension.
Find the book ↗
Castes of Mind
Nicholas Dirks, 2001 — How British colonialism transformed and hardened caste. Essential for historical context.
Find the book ↗
Ground Down by Growth
Alpa Shah et al., 2018 — Adivasi and Dalit experiences of India's growth story. Rigorous, data-driven.
Find the book ↗
Dalit Women Speak Out
Aloysius Irudayam et al., 2011 — Testimonies of Dalit women on caste violence and sexual assault. Primary source.
