Annihilation of Caste

An undelivered presidential address prepared for the Jat-Pat-Todak Mandal conference at Lahore, 1936. Self-published by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar.

What you are about to read was never spoken aloud as its author intended. When the organisers of the conference read his draft, they asked him to soften it. He refused. The conference was cancelled. He published the speech himself — along with the full correspondence with the organisers — so the world would know not only what he had written but why it was suppressed.

This edition is taken from the official Government of India publication of Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar's Writings and Speeches, Volume 1, compiled by Vasant Moon for the Education Department, Government of Maharashtra.

How this edition is structured

The text is organised into forty-three chapters, in four parts:

Preliminaries — the prefaces to the second and third editions, and the Prologue, which reproduces the full correspondence between Ambedkar and the Jat-Pat-Todak Mandal leading up to the cancellation.

The Address — twenty-five numbered sections, the speech itself.

Appendix I — Mahatma Gandhi's three articles written in the Harijan in response to the speech.

Appendix II — Ambedkar's reply to the Mahatma, in eleven sections.
